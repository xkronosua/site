Welcome to the Advanced Material Optical Diagnostics Lab!

We study nonlinear optical properties of various materials.

Nonlinear Optics Department of Institute of Physics (the oldest physical institution in Ukraine) is a well-known research center in the field of electronic and optical (especially nonlinear optical) properties of semiconductors, layered crystals, photorefractive and low-dimensional materials. Our team is a new Advanced Materials Optical Diagnostics (AMOD) group that are investigating nanoporous materials (TiO2 and TiO2 based nanocomposites prepared with different - sol-gel process, magnetron sputtering, screen printing - techniques, porous silicon etc.) and novel highly efficient NLO organic dyes (polymethine dye derivatives) films and guest-host systems. We are studying a correlation between the NLO response (photoinduced absorption/bleaching, NLO refractive index variation, generation of 2nd and 3rd harmonics) with electron structure, physical and chemical properties of the materials. The most promising result is a correlation of the effective cubic nonlinearity af anatase films dealing with a deep level resonant excitation with their photocatalytic activity.

Research

We are experienced in spectral analysis (transmission and reflectance spectra), angle resolved scattering measurements, low-temperature and vacuum technique and experimental data treatment (absorption and refractive index dispersion, modelling in a effective media approximation, estimation of the NLO response etc.).

AMOD equipment

Picosecond (20 ps, FWHM) and nanosecond (10 ns) Nd:YAG laser with harmonics conversion (1064, 532 and 355 nm)
Spectral setup based on grating monochromator MDR-12
Z-scan setup for NLO measurements
Vacuum and optical cryostat technique
Angle resolved scattering measurements setup based on goniometer G-5 (at 633 nm)
1024 pixels CCD-array AMKO LTI MULTIray
Computers and an original software
